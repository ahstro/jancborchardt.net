Students & free software

Let’s connect open source software projects with usability students!

There are many courses in university where students work on projects just for the sake of it – or even on proprietary projects, which do all their design themselves and will not be interested in external results anyway.

Then there are open source projects which oftentimes lack design contributors.

In bringing together open source projects with usability students, both parties benefit: The students get real experience, learn about open source and can say they worked on a project which many people use. Even if they don’t have that much design experience, the projects get more design input and maybe even permanent contributors.

The hardest part of contributing to an open source project is getting acommodated to the culture, knowing the communication channels and getting accepted by the team as a valuable contributor.

That’s why there should be a mentor, ideally both a usability expert and involved with the projects. Communication between the project and the student would initially be handled by that person – if the students like to and have time, they can also join the mailing list and IRC channel.

Comparable programs are Google Summer of Code and OpenUsability Season of Usability. These create external programs though. Instead, we should just use the existing educational framework of universities.

I did this already in Summer 2012 at my former university (Stuttgart Media University), in the course user experience research Methods led by Prof. Dr. Michael Burmester. Essentially I just went there and presented ownCloud, MediaGoblin and Bewelcome and offered the students to help them with their questions and connect them with the projects. Through that, 5 groups of three students each worked on these projects!

This should be an initiative that people can do independently at their universities.
