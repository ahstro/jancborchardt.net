GNOME design improvements

I love GNOME, especially the simplicity of GNOME Shell, and the folks on the design team are great people furthering an open design process. There are a few things which can be improved though. Some of these points are already possible to modify and test through installing extensions, others are ideas.

Overview

Open windows can’t be searched include open windows in search results maybe when there are no windows open in Shell, it still by default shows the »Windows« view. Instead, it should show the Applications view. the dash should be mainly opt-out, not mainly opt-in. Frequently used apps should automatically be put in it, and then autosorted by frequency+recency of use.

Top Panel

Hot Corner

The hot corner is a major problem because the top left corner is an easy location to accidentally move with your mouse. Important elements from an app are usually in the top left: First tab in a browser, mail app inbox, first file, …

I caught myself making an all-or-nothing design suggestion of removing the hot corner functionality altogether. When I talked to Allan, he mentioned they are working on a more intelligent hot corner which is activated depending on the force the cursor.

If you still want to disable it for now, you can use the extension No Topleft Hot Corner. The overview can still be reached by clicking the Activities button.

shut down vs suspend, learn from peoples behavior and switch it around, or just show it next to one another or show suspend in the shut down menu dont show doviders in log out menu when only 4 entries replace unavailable satus menu with option to add messenger account

Workspaces

I programmed the extension Remove Workspaces Sidebar because I don’t use workspaces at all. Previously and on other systems, I needed to always remove the workspace indicator, or reduce the number of workspaces to 0. In GNOME Shell the design is so unobtrusive if you don’t use workspaces that you don’t even have to care. There’s always an empty workspace, no need to manually add or remove any, and the whole area just peeks out a slight bit on the right edge of the overview. That’s why I just discontinued the extension.
